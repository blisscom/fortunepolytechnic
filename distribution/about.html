HISTOTY OF FORTUNE POLYTECHNIC

The journey of Fortune Schools System to attain the status of a tertiary institution commenced in 2001 with establishment of Fortune College of Management and Computer Technology. The college offered courses in various areas of computer operations and management. International Centre for Self-Management, Leadership and Strategic Studies was later set up to organize performance improvement workshops and short courses for senior employees in the public and private sectors. In 2014, the plan for Fortune Polytechnic was initiated. With the provision necessary resources, the institution is prepared for admission for the 2023/2024 session.

ADMISSION POLICY

The Polytechnic is open to all applicants without regards for race, religion, state, Local Government Area of Origin, sex or Political affiliation. Students shall be admitted into the National Diploma (ND) Programmes through the Joint Admission and Matriculation Board (JAMB) Examination only. However, obtaining Jamb offer does not translate to automatic admission into the Polytechnic as other conditions like satisfying the entry requirement of five (5) credits including English Language and Mathematics for the particular programme. All candidates admitted must attain a minimum age of sixteen as at the first day of reopening of the Polytechnic for the academic session. There is no maximum age limit.

RESUMPTION AND REGISTRATION

The Polytechnic had adopt two reopening dates during every first semester. For first year ND students, the dates of reopening and registration will be determined by the Academic Board on the release of admission letters by JAMB. Students must register for these programmes in person within TWO WEEKS of the commencement of each semester. Student will be registered only when they present the following documents

  1. The original letter of admission issued by the (JAMB) and approved to be rightly issued for ND Programmes or original letter of admission issued by the Polytechnic for the Preliminary or HND Programmes.
  2. Evidence of payment of the relevant institutional fees.
  3. Original Educational Certificates or certified photocopies of the computer print-out of WAEC/GCE O’Level/SSCE/NECO/NABTEB or DIPLOMA as may be applicable and meeting departmental entry requirements.
  4. Recent Medical certificate of fitness.
  5. Evidence of State and Local Government Area of origin.
  6. Four passport size photographs.
  7. Birth Certificate or sworn declaration of age.
  8. Evidence of good conduct from Principal of last school attended/employer/reputable citizens/senior public servant.
  9. Student applying for HND admission must present transcript of ND result before registration

NORMAL AND LATE REGISTRATION

Normal Registration

All students are required to register on admission and at the beginning of the semester. Students must register for their respective Programmes in person within two weeks of the beginning of the semester. A student is regarded as having registered for a Programme on the date he completes the registration formalities which include payment of prescribed fees and other charged; filling the registration forms and submitting same to the Head of Department and Academic affairs.

Late Registration

Any registration which is not complete within the first two weeks of registration shall be subject to extra charges as prescribes by the Academic Board. Such student will have to pay the stipulated late registration fees. Late registration shall last for two weeks. Any student who fails to register for his/her courses before the closing date for late registration shall neither be allowed to attend classes nor entered for the Semester Examination. Such a student shall be deemed to have voluntarily withdrawn from the Polytechnic and may also be re-admitted in any subsequent session or semester with the approval of the Academic Board. The Bursar shall cause to be published the list of students who have paid all stipulated fees at the end of the period of late registration.
